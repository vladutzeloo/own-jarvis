# Deep Research

Multi-source investigation with synthesis. Use when Vladimir needs to understand a topic well enough to make a decision or explain it to someone else.

## Trigger

- "Research X"
- "Help me understand Y"
- "What's the state of Z"
- Anything where one search is clearly not enough

## When NOT to use

- Quick factual lookups → [[03_Workflows/research/quick-lookup]]
- Vladimir already has the sources and just wants synthesis → skip the search phase

## Steps

**1. Frame the question.** Restate what's being asked in one sentence. Identify the decision or output it serves. If it's underspecified, ask one clarifying question, then proceed.

**2. Map the territory.** A first broad web search to see what kind of sources exist (academic, vendor, journalistic, forum). This shapes how to search next, not what to conclude.

**3. Triangulate.** For any contested claim, find at least two independent sources. For consensus claims, one good source is fine. Don't trust the first result.

**4. Read the strong sources end-to-end.** Skim the weak ones. Strong = primary documents, named experts, recent and specific. Weak = SEO blog posts, vendor whitepapers (read for marketing claims, not facts).

**5. Synthesize, don't summarize.** The output should answer the framing question, not list what was read. Lead with the answer; the evidence supports it.

**6. Mark uncertainty.** Where sources disagree, say so. Where the evidence is thin, say so.

**7. Save the output.** If it's reusable, file it under [[05_Knowledge/README]]. If it's project-bound, attach it to the project. Always include source links.

## Verification step

Before delivering: pick one non-obvious claim from the synthesis and re-verify it against the source. If it falls apart, the rest may too.
