# Summarize Thread

Distill a long email or Slack thread into something readable.

## Trigger

- "What's going on in this thread"
- "Catch me up on X"
- "TL;DR this for me"

## Steps

**1. Read the whole thread.** Don't shortcut — the structure of disagreement is often in the middle.

**2. Identify roles.** Who proposed what, who pushed back, who decided.

**3. Output structure:**
   - **Bottom line:** one sentence — what was decided, what's open, or what's the question
   - **Positions:** each named participant's stance in one line
   - **Open questions:** what's still unresolved
   - **Action items:** if any, with owner and timing

**4. Length.** A 50-message thread becomes a half-page. If your summary is longer than 200 words, you're probably re-narrating instead of distilling.

## Anti-patterns

- Summarizing message-by-message — you're not paid by the line.
- Hedging on what was decided when the thread is clear.
- Leaving out the disagreement — it's often the most important part.
